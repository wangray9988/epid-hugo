The Baudouin M33 Family — Propulsion vs Generator Versions</h2><p >The M33 engine platform from Baudouin (a subsidiary of Weichai Power since 2009) serves dual roles in the marine sector:</p><ul ><li ><strong >6M33P / 6M33 Marine Propulsion (this guide):</strong> Inline-6, 32.76L, optimized for continuous-duty propulsion with classification society certifications (BV, DNV, CCS, RINA, ABS available). Rated for 450–735 HP at 1,500–1,800 rpm. Designed for direct shaft drive or reduction gearbox coupling.</li><li ><strong >6M33G / 12M33G Marine Generator:</strong> Same base engine, optimized for constant-speed generator duty at 1,500/1,800 rpm. Different governor calibration, cooling circuit configuration, and classification requirements. <a  href="https://dieselengineparts.top/baudouin-m33-generator-engine-guide/">See our M33 Generator Engine Guide for details.</a></li></ul><div  class="spec-box"><strong >⚠️ Critical Distinction — Propulsion vs Generator Parts:</strong> While the base engine components (block, crankshaft, pistons, liners) are identical between propulsion and generator versions, several subsystems differ significantly: the governor/fuel pump calibration, the cooling system (propulsion often uses keel cooling or box coolers; generators use radiator arrangements), the turbocharger match (propulsion requires broader torque curve; generator needs flat speed regulation), and the flywheel/flywheel housing (SAE size differs for alternator vs gearbox mounting).
