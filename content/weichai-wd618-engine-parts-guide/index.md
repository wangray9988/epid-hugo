WD618 Series Overview: The Big Brother of WD615</h2><p >The WD618 was developed by Weichai Power in the late 1990s as a higher-displacement variant of the successful WD615 platform. While the WD615 displaced 9.726 liters, the WD618 stretched to 11.596 liters by increasing the bore from 126 mm to 126 mm and the stroke from 130 mm to 155 mm. This additional displacement delivered a substantial torque increase without requiring a V-engine configuration — an important distinction for maintenance simplicity and parts interchangeability.</p><p >Key design features shared across the WD618 family include:</p><ul ><li ><strong >Inline 6-cylinder, 4-stroke</strong> configuration with water cooling and turbocharging</li><li ><strong >Wet cylinder liners</strong> — replaceable and cost-effective for rebuilds</li><li ><strong >Direct injection</strong> combustion system with Bosch in-line fuel pump (P-type) on earlier models, transitioning to electronic unit pumps on later variants</li><li ><strong >Cast iron cylinder block</strong> with deep-skirt design for rigidity</li><li ><strong >Forged steel crankshaft</strong> with induction-hardened journals for extended service life</li><li ><strong >Overhead valve (OHV)</strong> layout with single camshaft</li><li ><strong >Gear-driven</strong> camshaft, oil pump, and water pump — no timing belt replacement concerns</li></ul><h2 >2. Use MLS gaskets and re-torque heads after the first 50 hours. Monitor coolant temperature closely.</li><li ><strong >Crankshaft Vibration Damper Degradation:</strong> The rubber-bonded damper on the front of the crankshaft deteriorates over time. A failed damper causes torsional vibration that can crack the crankshaft. Replace every 8,000 hours regardless of appearance.</li><li ><strong >Sea Water Pump Impeller Failure (Marine):</strong> The neoprene impeller degrades rapidly if run dry. Always open the sea cock before starting. Carry 2 spare impellers on-board at all times.</li><li ><strong >Fuel Injection Pump Governor Instability:</strong> On mechanical P-type pumps, governor spring fatigue can cause hunting (RPM fluctuation) at low speed. Recalibrate at 2,000-hour intervals.</li><li ><strong >Oil Cooler Internal Leakage:</strong> The plate-type oil cooler can develop internal leaks, allowing coolant to enter the oil system.
