Fuel System, Cooling & Lubrication Deep Dive</h2><h3 >6.1 HPCR Fuel System</h3><p >The WP10 uses a Bosch common-rail system operating near 1,600 bar. The solenoid-actuated injectors demand clean diesel and correct rail pressure. Contaminated fuel is the leading cause of premature injector and high-pressure-pump failure. EPID recommends a primary water-separating prefilter plus a 10-micron final filter, replaced every 10,000–15,000 km. When a pump fails, metal debris often migrates to the injectors — replace the pump and have injectors tested together.</p><h3 >6.2 Cooling System</h3><p >Turbocharged diesels reject significant heat through the coolant. In tropical and dusty sites, the water pump, thermostat and radiator must be kept clean. Use a nitrite/molybdate SCA additive to prevent liner cavitation, and pressure-test the cooling system at every major service. Overheating is the fastest route to a cracked head or seized piston on the WP10.</p><h3 >6.3 Lubrication</h3><p >The gear-type oil pump maintains pressure across the bearing gallery. Long service intervals without oil analysis lead to sludge that starves the turbo and bearings. Use CH-4 or CI-4 grade oil, sample at every change, and watch for rising iron (bearing wear) or silicon (dust ingress) trends that signal incoming failures.</p><h2 >7.
