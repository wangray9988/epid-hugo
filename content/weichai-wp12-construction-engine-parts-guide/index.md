Inline-Six Architecture Explained</h2><p >The WP12 arranges six cylinders in a single inline bank with a single four-valve cylinder head, dual overhead cams (or gear-driven valvetrain depending on variant) and a wet-liner block. The forged crankshaft runs in generous main bearings sized for continuous shock loading — the kind a loader or dozer delivers every cycle. Wet liners allow in-frame cylinder replacement without block machining, and the HPCR system maintains injection pressure for clean, low-smoke combustion under variable load.</p><h2 >3.
