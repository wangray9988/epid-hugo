Fuel System, Cooling & Lubrication Deep Dive</h2><h3 >6.1 HPCR Fuel System</h3><p >The WP4/WP6/WP7 use a Bosch high-pressure common-rail system operating near 1,600 bar. The injector is a solenoid-actuated unit that demands clean diesel and correct rail pressure. Contaminated fuel is the leading cause of premature injector failure; EPID recommends a primary water-separating prefilter and a 10-micron final filter, replaced every 250 hours.</p><h3 >6.2 Cooling System</h3><p >Turbocharged diesels reject significant heat through the coolant. The WP-series water pump, thermostat and radiator must be kept clean — particularly in tropical and dusty sites. Use a nitrite/molybdate SCA additive to prevent liner cavitation, and test coolant concentration every 1,000 hours.</p><h3 >6.3 Lubrication</h3><p >The gear-type oil pump maintains pressure across the bearing gallery. Long service intervals without oil analysis lead to sludge that starves the turbo and bearings. Use CH-4 or CI-4 grade oil, sample at every change, and watch for rising iron or silicon (dust) trends that signal incoming failures.</p><h2 >7.
