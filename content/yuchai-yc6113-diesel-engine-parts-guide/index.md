Inline-Six Architecture Explained</h2><p >The YC6113 arranges six cylinders in a single inline bank with a single cylinder head, a gear-driven cam and a wet-liner block. The inline layout simplifies balancing and maintenance — there is one head, one turbo and one injection pump to service. Wet liners let a workshop replace a scored cylinder without machining the block, which keeps in-frame overhauls fast and cheap. A gear-driven front end (rather than a belt) improves long-term timing reliability under load.</p><h2 >3.
