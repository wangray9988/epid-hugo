V16 Architecture Explained</h2><p >The 16M arranges sixteen cylinders in two banks of eight at a 60° vee. Each bank shares a cylinder head, a turbocharger and a cam profile, while a single high-pressure common-rail feeds all injectors. This modular bank design means a cylinder-head or turbo repair can be isolated to one bank, limiting teardown. The forged crankshaft runs in main bearings sized for continuous load, and the wet-liner design allows in-frame liner replacement without removing the block — a major advantage for remote-site overhauls.</p><h2 >3.
