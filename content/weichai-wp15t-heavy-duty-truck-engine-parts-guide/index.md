WP15T Architecture Highlights</h2><h3 >3.1 Block & Rotating Assembly</h3><p >The WP15T uses a CGI (compacted graphite iron) cylinder block — a material upgrade from the gray cast iron used in the WP13 — providing approximately 75% higher tensile strength and 40% higher fatigue strength. This enables the block to handle the 3,200 N-m peak torque without excessive weight. The deep-skirt design extends below the crankshaft centerline, with a ladder-frame main bearing cap structure that ties all seven main bearing caps together for maximum bottom-end rigidity under high cylinder pressure. The forged steel crankshaft features induction-hardened fillets and journals, with large-diameter main bearings (100+ mm) to handle the continuous high-load operation typical of heavy-haul duty cycles.</p><h3 >3.2 Fuel System</h3><p >The WP15T employs Bosch's latest-generation high-pressure common-rail system operating at up to 2,500 bar, with piezoelectric injectors capable of up to seven injection events per cycle (pilot, pre, main, post, and after-injection for DPF regeneration support). The high injection pressure combined with optimized combustion bowl geometry in the steel pistons enables the WP15T to achieve China VI emission limits without excessive EGR rates — reducing the soot-vs-NOx trade-off that plagues earlier-generation emission engines.</p><h3 >3.3 Air System & Turbocharging</h3><p >A Holset or Weichai-BorgWarner joint-venture VGT (variable geometry turbocharger) provides the boost, with electronic actuator control integrated into the engine ECU. The VGT's ability to vary turbine geometry enables strong low-end torque (peak torque available from 950 rpm) while avoiding boost pressure overshoot at high engine speeds. The air-to-air charge air cooler reduces intake manifold temperature to within 20–30 degrees C of ambient — critical for controlling NOx formation.</p><h2 >4.
